Statistical Methods
Volcano Plot — Differential Expression
Welch’s t-test — Two-sample unequal-variance t-test comparing group means. Degrees of freedom estimated via the Welch–Satterthwaite equation. P-values are computed from the regularized incomplete beta function.
Benjamini–Hochberg FDR — Multiple-testing correction controlling the false discovery rate. Adjusted p-values (q-values) are computed by ranking raw p-values and scaling by n/rank.
S0 correction — A fudge factor added to the t-test denominator (SE + S0) to penalize fold changes driven by low-abundance noise (Tusher et al., 2001). At S0 = 0 this is a standard t-test. Typical values range from 0.1–2.0.
Missing value imputation — Four methods available:
• Perseus-style Gaussian (default): draws from a down-shifted normal distribution in log2 space (shift=1.8 SD, width=0.3 SD). Ref: Tyanova et al. (2016) Nature Protocols 11:2301.
• MinProb: draws from a Gaussian centered at the 1st percentile of the log2 distribution. Ref: Lazar et al. (2016) J. Proteome Res. 15:1116.
• kNN: imputes from k=10 nearest neighbor proteins by Euclidean distance. Ref: Troyanskaya et al. (2001) Bioinformatics 17:520.
• Min/2: replaces with half the minimum non-zero value (simple deterministic baseline).
Log2 Fold Change — Calculated as log2(mean_X / mean_Y). Positive values indicate higher abundance in Group X; negative values indicate higher abundance in Group Y.
PCA — Principal Component Analysis
Algorithm — Dual-space PCA (kernel method) optimized for datasets where the number of samples is much smaller than the number of features. Computes the n×n kernel matrix K = X·XT from the mean-centered data matrix, then performs Jacobi eigendecomposition.
Scores — PC scores are eigenvectors scaled by the square root of their eigenvalues. Variance explained is reported as a percentage of total variance for each component.

Statistical Methods Used ▸
Volcano Plot
Test: Welch’s t-test (two-sample, unequal variance). Degrees of freedom via Welch–Satterthwaite approximation.
Multiple testing: Benjamini–Hochberg FDR correction. Adjusted p-values = raw_p × n / rank.
S0 correction: When enabled, a fudge factor S0 is added to the standard error denominator (SE + S0) to penalize noisy fold changes at low abundance (Tusher et al., 2001; Perseus).
Imputation: Four methods available — Perseus-style Gaussian (Tyanova et al. 2016), MinProb (Lazar et al. 2016), kNN (Troyanskaya et al. 2001), or Min/2 (simple). Default: Perseus-style.
Fold change: log2(mean_GroupX / mean_GroupY).
PCA
Method: Dual-space PCA via kernel matrix K = X·XT with Jacobi eigendecomposition. Optimized for n_samples << n_features.
Preprocessing: Column mean centering. No additional scaling.
Output: PC scores (eigenvectors × √eigenvalue). Variance explained as % of total.
